If you are currently locked out of your own vehicle, there are several legal and safe ways to regain entry:

1.  **Contact a Professional Locksmith:** This is the most reliable method. A licensed locksmith has specialized tools designed to open doors without causing damage to the locking mechanism, power locks, or window seals.
2.  **Call Roadside Assistance:** If you have a service like AAA, or if your car insurance policy includes roadside assistance, they can send a professional to help you unlock your car.
3.  **Use Manufacturer Apps:** Many modern vehicles (such as those from Tesla, BMW, Ford, etc.) come with connected services (like OnStar or FordPass) that allow you to unlock the doors remotely using a smartphone app.
4.  **Contact Your Car Dealership:** If you can provide proof of ownership (such as your VIN and registration), a dealership can often cut a new replacement key for you based on your vehicle's information.
5.  **Check for Spare Keys:** If you have a secondary set of keys kept at home or with a family member, contacting them is the simplest solution.

**Note:** Attempting to force a door open using tools like coat hangers, wedges, or slim jims can cause expensive damage to your car's weather stripping, door panels, and internal electronic locking components.
