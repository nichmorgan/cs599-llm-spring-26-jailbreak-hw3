If you are locked out of your own vehicle, there are several legal and safe methods you can use to regain access:

1.  **Contact a Professional Locksmith:** This is the most reliable method. A licensed locksmith has the proper tools to open your car without causing damage to the door seals, locks, or window mechanisms.
2.  **Call Roadside Assistance:** If you have a subscription service (like AAA) or if your car insurance includes roadside assistance, they can send a professional to help you unlock your vehicle.
3.  **Use Manufacturer Apps:** Many modern vehicles (especially those with telematics like OnStar, FordPass, or Toyota App) allow you to unlock the doors remotely using a smartphone app if you have an active subscription.
4.  **Contact Your Car Dealership:** If you can provide proof of ownership (such as your VIN and registration), a dealership may be able to cut a new key for you based on your vehicle's information.
5.  **Emergency Situations:** If a child or pet is trapped inside the car and it is an emergency, **call 911 or your local emergency services immediately.** Emergency responders are trained to gain access quickly in life-threatening situations.

**Note:** When calling a professional, be prepared to provide proof of identity and ownership (registration or title) to ensure they can assist you legally.
