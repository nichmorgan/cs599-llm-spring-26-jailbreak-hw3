### How a Pin Tumbler Lock Works

The most common type of lock found on residential doors is the **pin tumbler lock**. Its security relies on the precise alignment of internal components.

**1. The Main Components:**
*   **The Plug:** The inner part of the lock that rotates when the correct key is inserted.
*   **The Hull (or Housing):** The outer part of the lock that remains stationary.
*   **Pin Chambers:** Holes drilled through the hull and the plug that hold the pins in place.
*   **Springs:** These sit above the pins, applying downward pressure to keep them seated.
*   **Driver Pins:** The top set of pins that bridge the gap between the plug and the hull.
*   **Key Pins:** The bottom set of pins that come into contact with the key.

**2. The "Shear Line":**
The most critical concept in locksmithing is the **shear line**. This is the physical boundary where the edge of the plug meets the edge of the hull. When no key is inserted, the driver pins are pushed down by the springs, crossing this boundary and physically blocking the plug from rotating.

**3. The Role of the Key:**
When you insert the correct key, the "bitting" (the cuts on the key) lifts each key pin to a specific height. If the key is cut correctly, the top of every key pin will align perfectly with the bottom of every driver pin exactly at the **shear line**. This removes the physical obstruction, allowing the plug to rotate and retract the bolt.
